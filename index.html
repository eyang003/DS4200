Climate Change and Agriculture

This website presents an analysis of the impact of climate change on agriculture across different regions and countries from 1990 to 2024. As global concerns over food insecurity, climate change, and ecosystem challenges continue to grow, agriculture stands at the intersection of these critical issues. The sector is inherently dependent on natural resources, such as land and water, that are highly sensitive to shifts in weather patterns and climate conditions. The objective of this analysis is to explore the relationships between environmental factors, such as temperature fluctuations, precipitation levels, and CO2 emissions, and agricultural outcomes, including crop yields. To support this, we are using a comprehensive dataset that spans more than three decades and incorporates data from various countries and a diverse range of crop types, such as barley, rice, corn, and fruits. Through this analysis, we aim to uncover trends and patterns that illustrate how climate change has influenced agricultural practices and outcomes over time. Ultimately, this work contributes to the ongoing conversation on sustainable agriculture and food security in an increasingly unpredictable climate.

Data Introduction

The dataset used in this analysis was sourced from Kaggle, and it includes data from 1990 to 2024, covering various regions and crops across the world. It contains 10,000 observations on climate and agricultural trends, including factors like temperature, precipitation, crop yield, CO2 emissions, and extreme weather events. Below is a summary of the key attributes in the dataset:

Variable Name Details
Year Year of the data record (1990-2024)
Country Country where the data is collected

Crop_Type Type of crop (e.g., wheat, rice, corn)

CO2_Emissions_MT Total CO2 emissions (in metric tons) for the year

Pesticide_Use_KG_per_HA Amount of pesticide used (in kg per hectare)
Fertilizer_Use_KG_per_HA Amount of fertilizer used (in kg per hectare)
Soil_Health_Index Index of soil health (0-100 scale)
Adaptation_Strategies Adaptation strategy used (e.g., crop rotation, drought-resistant crops)
Economic_Impact_Million_USD Economic impact of climate change (in million USD)

Summary of Analysis

Through the analysis of this dataset, we have explored how climate change has affected agriculture across different countries and regions. The analysis identifies key trends in temperature increase, precipitation changes, and the frequency of extreme weather events, all of which have a direct impact on crop yields and farming practices. In addition, the data reveals insights into the adoption of adaptation strategies and their role in mitigating these effects.
